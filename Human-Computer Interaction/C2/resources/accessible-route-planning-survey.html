User Testing Survey - Public Transport App Accessibility

Introduction

Hello, and thank you for participating in this user testing session!

Before we begin, I want to highlight that we are testing the application and not you. There are no right or wrong answers, and your honest feedback is extremely valuable to us. This session will help us understand how to improve public transportation applications to make them more accessible and user-friendly.

This session will take approximately 45-50 minutes, you only need to complete a few tasks using the Public Transport App application and then complete the survey.

Preliminary Questions

First, I'd like to ask you a few simple questions:

PART 1: Accessible Route Planning
Proposed Scenario: "You want to visit a friend who lives in the city center for a coffee. You haven't been there in a while, and you want to make sure the route is easy for you to take."

Tasks to attempt:

Task 1: Open Public Transport App and search for a route from your home location to the city center.
Task 2: Check if you can apply any filters to make sure your route avoids stairs and includes ramps or elevators.
Task 3: Look at the different route options, select the one that seems most accessible and then start the navigation.

Survey - Complete the following questions based on your experience

1. How easy was it to find the route planning feature in Public Transport App?
2. Were you able to find the accessibility options for route planning?
3. Which of these accessibility filters were you able to find? (select all that apply)
4. How clear was the information provided about route accessibility?
5. Were you able to compare different route options based on accessibility?
6. Did the app provide information about accessible entrances at your destination?
7. Were you able to start navigation for your chosen accessible route?
8. What accessibility barriers did you encounter during this task? (select all that apply)
What improvements would you suggest for the accessible route planning feature?
PART 2: Configurable Alerts
Proposed Scenario: "You started to use this app regularly to visit your doctor. Since there are often changes in elevator availability and service times, you want the app to keep you informed automatically so you don't get surprised by service disruptions"

Tasks to attempt:

Task 1: Open Public Transport App and find and find where you can configure the alert settings.
Task 2: Try to set up alerts that will notify you if the route changes or if there's a disruption, especially one that affects elevators or access points.
Task 3: Try to simulate or test one of the alerts, to see if it works properly and you will notice it during your trip.

Survey

1. Were you able to find the alert configuration settings in Public Transport App?
2. Which of these alert types were you able to configure? (select all that apply)
3. Could you customize how you receive alerts? (select all that apply)
4. Were you able to set up alerts specifically for accessibility features (like elevator outages)?
5. Were you able to successfully test or simulate an alert?
6. If you tested an alert, was it noticeable enough for someone with disabilities or impairments?
7. How easy was it to save your alert configurations?
8. What accessibility barriers did you encounter when trying to configure alerts? (select all that apply)
What improvements would you suggest for the alert system to better serve users with accessibility needs?
Overall Feedback
1. Based on your experience, how would you rate Public Transport App for accessibility on a scale of 1-5?
2. What was the most difficult aspect of using Public Transport App with the simulated disability?
3. What features would you like to see added to make Public Transport App more accessible?
