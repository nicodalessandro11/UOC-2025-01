User Testing Survey - Google Maps Accessibility

1. Introduction

Hello, and thank you for participating in this user testing session!

Before we begin, I want to highlight that we are testing the application and not you. There are no right or wrong answers, and your honest feedback is extremely valuable to us. This session will help us understand how to improve public transportation applications to make them more accessible and user-friendly.

This session will take approximately 45-50 minutes, you only need to complete a few tasks using the Google Maps application and then complete the survey.

2. Test Case: Accessible Route Planning

Proposed Scenario: "You want to visit a friend who lives in the city center for a coffee. You require wheelchair accessibility and would like to find a route that meets your needs."

Tasks to attempt:

Task 1: Open Google Maps and search for a route from your home location to the city center. Look for the wheelchair accessible routes option and enable it.
Task 2: Explore the accessible route information provided and check if it includes details about elevator availability, ramps, or other accessibility features.
Task 3: Find and use the "Report an accessibility issue" feature to report a hypothetical accessibility problem on the route

Survey - Complete the following questions based on your experience:

1. Were you able to find and enable the wheelchair accessible routes option?
2. How easy was it to find the wheelchair accessible route option in Google Maps?
3. What specific accessibility information was provided about the route? (select all that apply)
4. How clear was the information provided about route accessibility?
5. Were you able to find and use the "Report an accessibility issue" feature?
6. Did the app provide information about accessible entrances or facilities at your destination?
7. How would you rate the process of reporting an accessibility issue?
8. What accessibility barriers did you encounter during this task? (select all that apply)
What improvements would you suggest for the accessible route planning feature?
3. Test Case: Configurable Alerts
Proposed Scenario: "You use Google Maps regularly for your commute. You want to stay informed about transit updates, especially those that can affect accessibility, like elevator maintenance or service changes."

Tasks to attempt:

Task 1: Open Google Maps and try to find where to turn on notifications related to public transport.
Task 2: Search for a train or metro station and check if you can find real time accessibility information alerts or notifications like elevators or step-free access.
Task 3: Plan a trip using public transport and try to save the route. Check if there is any way to get updates or alerts for this route.

Survey - Complete the following questions based on your experience:

1. Were you able to find where to configure public transport notifications?
2. Which of these alert types were you able to configure? (select all that apply)
3. Could you customize how you receive alerts? (select all that apply)
4. Could you find a way to get alerts for transit delays or service issues on a specific route?
5. Was the notifications information easy to read and understand?
6. How useful were the updates or notifications for someone with accessibility needs?
7. What accessibility barriers did you encounter when trying to configure alerts? (select all that apply)
What improvements would you suggest for configurable alerts and transit updates to better serve users with accessibility needs?

4. Overall Feedback

1. Based on your experience, how would you rate Google Maps for accessibility on a scale of 1-5?
2. What was the most difficult aspect of using Google Maps?
3. What features would you like to see added to make Google Maps more accessible?
